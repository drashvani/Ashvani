Overview

About Dr. Patel

Dr. Ashvani Kumar Patel builds new, more sustainable routes to complex nitrogen-containing molecules. As an Assistant Professor in the Department of Applied Sciences & Humanities at Invertis University, Bareilly, he teaches chemistry while running a research program centred on organocatalyzed transformations that turn simple starting materials into pharmaceutically relevant heterocycles — pyridines, pyrazolones, isoxazoles, and related scaffolds — often without metal catalysts or solvent, in line with green chemistry principles.

His doctoral work at IIT Indore, carried out under Dr. Sampak Samanta, focused on devising efficient, atom-economical strategies for constructing value-added pyridine and pyrazolone frameworks. That training gave him deep, hands-on command of modern structural characterization — NMR, single-crystal X-ray diffraction, and high-resolution mass spectrometry — and a Rigaku School certification in crystallography that few early-career chemists carry into the classroom.

Beyond the lab, Dr. Patel is committed to student-centred teaching: building conceptual clarity, analytical reasoning, and problem-solving ability in undergraduate and postgraduate chemistry students, and mentoring them toward research and scientific curiosity.

Focus Areas

Research & Expertise

Dr. Patel's work sits in synthetic organic chemistry, with an emphasis on organocatalysis and sustainable methodology development — designing reactions that are shorter, cleaner, and more selective than the metal-catalysed routes they replace.

🧪

Organocatalysis

Metal-free catalytic strategies for constructing complex heterocyclic scaffolds with high selectivity.

🌱

Green Chemistry

Solvent-free and domino/one-pot reaction design for efficient, sustainable synthesis.

🔗

Heterocycle Synthesis

Pyridines, pyrazolones, isoxazoles, and indolizines built via cascade condensation and cyclization reactions.

🔬

Structural Characterization

NMR spectroscopy, Single Crystal X-Ray Diffraction (SC-XRD), and High-Resolution Mass Spectrometry (HRMS).
